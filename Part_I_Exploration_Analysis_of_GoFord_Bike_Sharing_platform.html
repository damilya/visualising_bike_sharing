Part I - Ford GoBike System Data¶

by Damilya Zhantayeva¶

Introduction¶

The dataset reprepresents individual bike rides on a bike sharing Ford GoBike platform. All rides were made in a greater San Francisco Bay area. Overall, there are 16 columns present in the dataset. They denote general information on usage of the bikes such as duration of each ride, start and end time, and on customers such as customer type, their year of birth and gender. We may not use all of the columns, but rather focus on some of the features.

Overview on the dataset¶

There are 183,412 rows. Impressive, seems like it is a popular platform, at least in San Francisco. As expected, there are a few missing data entries:

  • 1) start_station_id, start_station_name, end_station_id, end_station_name columns have around 200 missing values, and member_birth_year, member_gender have around 800 missing values.

We can also see some data type discrepancies:

  • 2) start_time, end_time are of dtype object, not datetime
  • 3) start_station_id, end_station_id, bike_id, member_birth_year are of dtype float or int, though no arithmetic operations can be done on them
  • 4) user_type, member_gender, bike_share_for_all_trip is of dtype object, not categorical

Do we need all columns?

  • 5) latitude and longtitude measures are very specific values, we may not want to analyse them closely in this project

A few clean-up touches should be performed on this dataset.

The main features of interest in the dataset¶

There are several interesting insights we can analyze in this dataset. It is great to have at least some information on the customers such as gender and year of birth. We could think about the following features:

  • 1) What is the distribution of the genders in a dataset?
  • 2) What is the distribution of the customers age?
  • 3) Which gender is more likely to purchase/use Subscription? Of what age?

We can also analyze the features of the rides:

  • 1) What is the distribution of the duration of each ride?
  • 2) How does the duration of each ride depend on the gender of a user?
  • 3) How does the duration of each ride depend on the age of a user?
  • 4) How does the duration of each ride depend on the user type?

There might be more features to explore, we will add up more on the way. Re-iteration is good to see some new insights.

We will focus on the duration pre ride, member age, user type, and member gender feature. Possibly we will extend our list of feature during the process.

Conclusions¶

In the first part Univariate Exploration, we analysed the distribution of single variables. We found out that on average the users mostly spent around 40 minutes per ride, while some extreme outliers were also found of 20-23 hours. In terms of distribution among the age of the users, the most active users are in their late 20s, early 30s. The youngest users are 18-19, and the oldest could not be determined due to data corruption. We also derived 3 genders: females, males, others; and 2 user types: customer and subsription.

In the second part Bivariate Exploration, we derived reationships among the values explored in the first part. While we previously found out that the users in their end 20s and early 30s are the most frequent users, they also contribute to the longest rides. The users aged 60 plus spent signficantly less time cycling. In general, gender does not affect the duration of each ride. However, Males contribute the most to the longest rides. For user types, a preference to be a Subscriber is prevailed among all 3 genders. But Males tend to be a Subscriber way more compared to other two genders.

In the last part Multivariate Exploration, we combined the relationship among the previously analysed variables. Customers of any gender take longer rides compared to Subscribers. Interestingly, females on average take longer rides regardless of their user type compared to other two genders. To finalyse our exploration, we checked more insights about the users. Even though, overall Males tend to use Subscription model, Males in their end 20s use the model way less compared to othe age groups of the same category. But Males aged 45 plus are the most consistent users among all genders of that age group. Other gender gave us an interesting observation, that Other Subsribers and Customers have an opposite to each other trend in their 50s and 60s.
